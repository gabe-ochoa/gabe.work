About

I started as a mechanical engineer at Georgia Tech and spent my first years on the prototyping bench at Quirky — turning sketches into things you could hold. That habit of building real, tangible products never left.

From there I moved into infrastructure and reliability, then engineering leadership: edge and platform teams at Squarespace, hardware-meets-AI at Numina, and now data observability at Datadog. The technology keeps changing; the work doesn't. I build teams that are high-performing because they're trusted, and I keep them pointed at problems that matter to real customers.

I care most about the seam where software touches the physical world — sensors, hardware, data that describes something actually happening out there — and about being the kind of manager people do their best work for.
